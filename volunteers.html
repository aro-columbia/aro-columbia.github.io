Volunteer Information

Important information and expectations for ARO volunteers.

Volunteer Expectations

Commitment

  • Attend your assigned sessions regularly and on time
  • Notify coordinators as soon as possible if you cannot attend
  • Commit to at least one semester of volunteering

Conduct

  • Be a positive role model for the students
  • Maintain appropriate boundaries with students at all times
  • Follow all site-specific rules and guidelines
  • Respect the staff, students, and facilities at partner sites

Communication

  • Check your email and group chats regularly for updates
  • Respond promptly to messages from coordinators
  • Report any concerns or incidents to coordinators immediately

Requirements

Background Check

All volunteers must complete a Columbia University background check. You will receive an email from HireRight with instructions. Complete this within 5 days of receiving the email.

Protection of Minors Training

You will receive an email from columbia@sabacloud.com assigning the Protection of Minors: Boundaries training. Complete this within 14 days of receiving the email.

Fingerprinting

All volunteers must get fingerprinted before volunteering with minors. This is required by NYC DOE.

  • A valid Social Security Number (SSN) is required
  • Schedule a 5-minute meeting with our advisor Cait to provide your information
  • ARO covers the fingerprinting cost - you'll receive a unique code
  • After fingerprinting, immediately take a photo of the receipt and send it to Cait (cmh2254@columbia.edu)

If you completed background check and fingerprinting last year, you do NOT need to do it again.

Training

All new volunteers must attend a required lesson planning training meeting at the beginning of the semester.

Schedule & Logistics

Sessions are typically held during the academic year (around 9 lessons per semester). Specific days and times vary by partner site. You will be assigned to a site and time slot based on your availability.

Travel to partner sites is usually by foot or public transit. Sites are located in the Morningside Heights and Harlem area, close to campus.

Supplies Pickup & Dropoff

Art supplies are stored in Earl Hall Room 204. Pick up your supplies on the way to class and return them after.

  • Each group has a labeled tote bag with requested supplies
  • Return supplies to the same room after class
  • If you accidentally keep supplies, let the coordinators know

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need art experience?

No! We welcome volunteers of all backgrounds. Lesson plans and materials are provided, and you'll have support from experienced volunteers.

What if I have to miss a session?

Let your coordinator know as soon as possible so we can arrange coverage. Occasional absences are understandable, but consistent attendance is important for building relationships with students.

Can I get involved if I don't have an SSN?

Unfortunately, as of 2026, NYC DOE requires an SSN for all volunteers in public schools. However, you may still be able to volunteer at non-DOE partner sites. Contact us for more information.

Who do I contact if I have questions?

Reach out to your site coordinator or email us at aro@communityimpact.columbia.edu.
