Volunteer Information

Important information and expectations for ARO volunteers.

Volunteer Expectations

Commitment

  • Attend your assigned sessions regularly and on time
  • Notify coordinators as soon as possible if you cannot attend
  • Commit to at least one semester of volunteering

Conduct

  • Be a positive role model for the students
  • Maintain appropriate boundaries with students at all times
  • Follow all site-specific rules and guidelines
  • Respect the staff, students, and facilities at partner sites

Communication

  • Check your email and group chats regularly for updates
  • Respond promptly to messages from coordinators
  • Report any concerns or incidents to coordinators immediately

Requirements

NYC DOE Clearance

All volunteers working with NYC public schools must complete the NYC Department of Education background check process. This requires:

  • A valid Social Security Number (SSN)
  • Completion of fingerprinting
  • PETS system registration

Coordinators will provide guidance on completing this process at the start of each semester.

Training

All new volunteers must attend an orientation session at the beginning of the semester. Additional training may be required by specific partner sites.

Schedule & Logistics

Sessions are typically held during the academic year. Specific days and times vary by partner site. You will be assigned to a site and time slot based on your availability.

Travel to partner sites is usually by foot or public transit. Sites are located in the Morningside Heights and Harlem area, close to campus.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need art experience?

No! We welcome volunteers of all backgrounds. Lesson plans and materials are provided, and you'll have support from experienced volunteers.

What if I have to miss a session?

Let your coordinator know as soon as possible so we can arrange coverage. Occasional absences are understandable, but consistent attendance is important for building relationships with students.

Can I get involved if I don't have an SSN?

Unfortunately, as of 2026, NYC DOE requires an SSN for all volunteers in public schools. However, you may still be able to volunteer at non-DOE partner sites. Contact us for more information.

Who do I contact if I have questions?

Reach out to your site coordinator or email us at aro@communityimpact.columbia.edu.
