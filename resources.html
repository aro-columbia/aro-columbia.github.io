Resources

Lesson plans, art resources, and educational materials for volunteers.

Required Lesson Topics

There are around 9 lessons per semester. Every class should cover these topics through art:

  • Harlem Renaissance (Required for all classes)

Plus choose 2 from this list:

  • English Language Arts
  • Mathematics
  • Science
  • Social Studies

Configure lessons based on grade level. See NYC Public School curriculum for guidance, and ask the teachers!

Lesson Development

Lesson Format

Each lesson plan should include:

  • Central Topic: The main theme or project
  • Lesson Goals: What students will learn
  • Organization of Class Time: How time is divided (e.g., 15 min presentation, 35 min activity)
  • Supplies Needed: List all materials
  • Member's Roles: Who does what

Lesson Tips

  • Try to introduce students to 2-3 new skills per lesson
  • Be niche - bring in lesser-known artists, movements, and art forms
  • Think about diversity and inclusion when choosing artists
  • If things aren't working out, try something else next lesson
  • The more detailed your lesson plan, the better prepared you'll be
  • Ask yourself if the lesson is meaningful

During Class

  • Bring handouts that introduce students to new artists, works, or themes
  • Ask students periodically what they want to learn
  • Ask teachers what they expect and connect to what students are learning
  • Get your teacher's contact info (email AND phone number)

Lesson Ideas

Drawing & Painting

  • Self-portraits using mirrors
  • Landscape painting from observation or imagination
  • Abstract art inspired by emotions or music
  • Still life with simple objects

Mixed Media & Collage

  • Magazine collage portraits
  • Texture collages using fabric, paper, and found materials
  • Vision boards

3D & Sculpture

  • Paper mache masks or bowls
  • Clay pinch pots and sculptures
  • Origami and paper folding
  • Cardboard construction

Seasonal & Cultural

  • Holiday cards and decorations
  • Art from different cultures and traditions
  • Nature-inspired projects (leaves, flowers, animals)

Art Supplies

Basic Supplies

You will get basic supplies for the first class:

  • Papers
  • Markers, pens, pastels
  • Additional supplies from our inventory upon request (not guaranteed)

Supply Request Process

Supply requests ask you to submit 3 lesson plans at a time via Google Form:

  • Include your group member's UNI
  • Classroom time/number
  • 3 PDFs of your lesson plans with supply lists

Budget & Vendors

  • Each class has a budget of around $20 for special supplies per lesson
  • You can only use this budget if you submit your lesson on time
  • Preferred vendors: Janoff, Westside Market, Amazon
  • Submit 3-4 weeks in advance for Amazon orders

Important Policies

There are no refunds on anything you buy for your ARO class. Everything must be purchased by the ARO team with a Community Impact-approved voucher. If you buy supplies yourself, we cannot refund you.

To change a supply request or request additional materials, email aro@communityimpact.columbia.edu and cc Quoc, José, and Conner.

Taking Care of Supplies

  • Clean paint brushes and palettes after use; throw away one-use items
  • Throw away unusable supplies (empty paint, dried markers, tiny crayon pieces)
  • If you throw anything away, message the GroupMe
