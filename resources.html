Lesson Development

Planning a Lesson

When developing lessons, consider:

  • Age appropriateness: Adjust complexity for preschool vs. middle school students
  • Time constraints: Most sessions are 45-60 minutes; plan accordingly
  • Materials: Check what's available at your site before planning
  • Learning objectives: What skill or concept should students take away?

Lesson Structure

A typical lesson includes:

  1. Introduction (5-10 min): Introduce the concept, show examples, demonstrate technique
  2. Activity (30-40 min): Guided hands-on art making
  3. Wrap-up (5-10 min): Share work, clean up, reflection

Lesson Ideas

Drawing & Painting

  • Self-portraits using mirrors
  • Landscape painting from observation or imagination
  • Abstract art inspired by emotions or music
  • Still life with simple objects

Mixed Media & Collage

  • Magazine collage portraits
  • Texture collages using fabric, paper, and found materials
  • Vision boards

3D & Sculpture

  • Paper mache masks or bowls
  • Clay pinch pots and sculptures
  • Origami and paper folding
  • Cardboard construction

Seasonal & Cultural

  • Holiday cards and decorations
  • Art from different cultures and traditions
  • Nature-inspired projects (leaves, flowers, animals)

Art Supplies Guide

Basic Supplies

Most sites have these materials available:

  • Construction paper (various colors)
  • Crayons, markers, colored pencils
  • Scissors (child-safe)
  • Glue sticks and liquid glue
  • Tempera paint and brushes
  • Pencils and erasers

Requesting Additional Materials

If your lesson requires special materials, contact your coordinator at least one week in advance. We can often provide or reimburse for supplies.
